References

A concise list of widely used histories and surveys. Editions and publishers vary; any recent printing of the title will serve as a starting point.

General histories of mathematics

  • Carl B. Boyer and Uta C. Merzbach, A History of Mathematics — a standard narrative survey from antiquity toward modern times.
  • Victor J. Katz, A History of Mathematics: An Introduction — textbook-style treatment with broad geographic coverage.
  • John Stillwell, Mathematics and Its History — connects mathematical ideas across periods with a problem-oriented feel.
  • Dirk J. Struik, A Concise History of Mathematics — compact classic, strong on social and intellectual context.
  • Morris Kline, Mathematical Thought from Ancient to Modern Times — expansive three-volume account of the development of mathematical ideas.

Indian mathematics and non-European traditions

  • Kim Plofker, Mathematics in India — authoritative historical survey of Indian mathematical practice and texts.
  • George Gheverghese Joseph, The Crest of the Peacock: Non-European Roots of Mathematics — argues for a wider geography of mathematical creativity, with substantial treatment of India.
