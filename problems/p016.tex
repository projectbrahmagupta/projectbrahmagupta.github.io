\begin{problem}{Prime Sign Sums}
    Ritabrata Munshi studied cancellation in sums over primes, a central theme in analytic number theory where large arithmetic sums remain surprisingly small because oscillations destroy accumulation. In this problem we make that phenomenon concrete and computational. We define:
    \[
    S(n) = \sum_{2 < p \leq n} (-1)^{(p-1)/2}
    \]
    where the sum runs over odd primes \(p\). Equivalently, \(S(n)\) adds \(+1\) for primes \(p \equiv 1 \pmod{4}\) and \(-1\) for primes \(p \equiv 3 \pmod{4}\). Find the smallest \(n\) for which \(|S(n)| > 100\).
    
\end{problem}
