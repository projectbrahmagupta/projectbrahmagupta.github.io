Baudhāyana, in the oldest of the Śulba Sūtras, states what we now call the Pythagorean theorem for a rectangle.

A primitive Pythagorean triple is a triple \((a,b,c)\) of positive integers with \(a^2+b^2=c^2\), \(\gcd(a,b,c)=1\), and \(a<b<c\). How many primitive Pythagorean triples have hypotenuse \(c\) at most \(1000\)?
