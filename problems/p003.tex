\begin{problem}{The House in Louvain}
Henry Dudeney's 1917 puzzle: on a long street in Louvain the houses are numbered $1, 2, 3, \ldots, n$ in sequence. A certain house, numbered $k$, has the property that the sum of all house numbers less than $k$ equals the sum of those greater than $k$. It is known that $50 < n < 500$.

Find $k$.
\end{problem}
