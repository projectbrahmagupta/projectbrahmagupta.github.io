\begin{problem}{Highly Composite Numbers}
    In 1915, Ramanujan studied integers with unusually many divisors. A positive integer is called \emph{highly composite} if it has more divisors than every smaller positive integer.

    Let \(d(n)\) denote the number of positive divisors of \(n\). Thus \(n\) is highly composite if
    \[
    d(n) > d(k)
    \qquad \text{for all } 1 \le k < n.
    \]

    For example, \(12\) is highly composite since \(d(12)=6,\) while every positive integer smaller than \(12\) has at most \(4\) divisors. Determine the smallest highly composite number having more than \(10^3\) divisors.
    
\end{problem}
