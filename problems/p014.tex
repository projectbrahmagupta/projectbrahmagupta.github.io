\begin{problem}{The AKS Impostor}

    The AKS primality test is based on the fact that every prime \(p\) satisfies
    \[
    (x-a)^p \equiv x^p-a \pmod p
    \]
    for all integers \(a\). However, certain composite integers can satisfy weakened forms of this congruence.
    Determine the smallest composite integer \(n\) such that
    \[
    (x-1)^n \equiv x^n-1
    \pmod{x^r-1,\; n}
    \]
    for every integer \(1 \le r \le 20\).
    \end{problem}
