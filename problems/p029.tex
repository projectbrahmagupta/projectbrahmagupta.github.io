\begin{problem}{Balanced Numbers}
    A natural number $N$ is balanced if it can be written as the sum of two adjacent blocks of consecutive natural numbers. That is, $N$ is balanced if there exist positive integers $a$ and $k$ such that
    \[
    N = a + (a+1) + \cdots + (a+k) = (a+k+1) + (a+k+2) + \cdots + (a+k'),
    \]
    where $k < k'$. For example, $15$ is balanced because $4 + 5 + 6 = 15 = 7 + 8$.

    How many balanced numbers are there less than $10^6$?
\end{problem}
