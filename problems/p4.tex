Mānava’s construction for a square equal in area to a circle of diameter \(d\) begins with
\[
  s \approx d - \frac{d}{8} + \frac{d}{8\cdot 29} - \frac{d}{8\cdot 29\cdot 6} + \frac{d}{8\cdot 29\cdot 6\cdot 8}.
\]
Take \(d=1\), let \(s\) be the resulting side, and set \(\pi_{\mathrm{Śulba}} = 4s^2\). Compute \(\lfloor 10^8 \cdot \pi_{\mathrm{Śulba}} \rfloor\).
