\begin{problem}{Faces of the Bhargava Cube}
Manjul Bhargava's work on higher composition laws reinterprets Gauss composition by placing eight integers at the corners of a $2 \times 2 \times 2$ cube; the three pairs of opposite faces yield binary quadratic forms whose product is the identity. For integers $a, b, c, d$, define the symmetric sum $$B(a, b, c, d) = ab + ac + ad + bc + bd + cd.$$ Call $(a, b, c, d)$ balanced if $B(a, b, c, d)$ is a perfect cube.

Find the number of balanced quadruples with $1 \leq a, b, c, d \leq 10^4$.
\end{problem}
