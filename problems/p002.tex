\begin{problem}{Vargaprakriti}
    The \(n\)th hexagonal number \(h_n\) is the number of distinct dots in a pattern of dots consisting of the outlines of regular hexagons with sides up to \(n\) dots, when the hexagons are overlaid so that they share one vertex. It is easy to show that

    \[
    h_n = n(2n - 1)
    \]

    The first hexagonal number \(h_1 = 1\) is a perfect square. Find the second smallest hexagonal number that is a perfect square.

    \fontcolor{gray}{FFT: How many such square-hexagonal numbers are there? What about cube-hexagonal numbers?}
\end{problem}
