\begin{problem}{Rational Diagonals}
    For a cyclic quadrilateral with consecutive side lengths \(a,b,c,d\), the diagonal lengths \(p,q\) satisfy
    \[
    p^2=\frac{(ab+cd)(ac+bd)}{ad+bc},
    \qquad
    q^2=\frac{(ad+bc)(ac+bd)}{ab+cd}.
    \]
    Although the product \(pq\) is always rational, the individual diagonals need not be. Determine the number of quadruples of distinct positive integers
    \[
    a\le b\le c\le d,
    \qquad
    a+b+c+d=1000,
    \]
    for which both diagonals are rational.
\end{problem}
