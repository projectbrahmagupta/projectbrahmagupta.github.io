\begin{problem}{Brahmagupta Quadrilaterals}
    In the 7th century, Brahmagupta gave a formula for the area of a cyclic quadrilateral (this extends Heron’s formula for triangles). If a cyclic quadrilateral has side lengths \(a,b,c,d\) and semiperimeter
    \[
    s=\frac{a+b+c+d}{2},
    \]
    then its area is
    \[
    A=\sqrt{(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)(s-d)}.
    \]
    A cyclic quadrilateral is called integral if all four side lengths and its area are integers. How many integral cyclic quadrilaterals satisfy \(a \le b \le c \le d,\) all four side lengths are distinct, and \(a+b+c+d \le 60\)?
\end{problem}
