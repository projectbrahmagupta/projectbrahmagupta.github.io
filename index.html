१०८  ·  mathematics and computation

Mathematics is learned by doing.

Project Brahmagupta is a collection of problems in mathematics and computation, inspired by the Indian tradition of ganita and by writers such as Brahmagupta, Bhāskara II, and Mahāvīra.

The problems are intended to be solved with pencil, argument, and code where it helps. Some are elementary; some are harder; most reward a clean solution rather than brute force.

Use any language you like. There is no affiliation requirement and no ranking table—only the problems.

During the run, one problem is listed each day at midnight India Standard Time. The archive lists everything published so far.

About

Project Brahmagupta grew out of an interest in the history of mathematics in India and in the problem traditions preserved across Sanskrit mathematical texts. Alongside their historical importance, many of these problems remain mathematically rich and computationally interesting even today.

The project aims to present this material in a form suitable for modern problem solving, while remaining attentive to the original sources and methods.

We are currently curating the initial collection of problems and related material. The project is expected to begin in mid-June 2026.

The structure of the project is inspired in part by Project Euler and similar recreational mathematics problem archives.

For a short shelf of standard references on the history of mathematics, including works that place Indian mathematics in a broader historical context, see References.
