१०८  ·  one hundred and eight problems

Daily problems from Indian mathematics.

Every morning, one problem from the Indian mathematical tradition — drawn from centuries of mathematics composed in Sanskrit, computed on sand, and preserved in verse. Each problem will require more than mathematical intuition alone — you will need to write code, think about efficiency, and often learn an idea you have never encountered before. The aim is to provide a place where curiosity about mathematics can be fed in a context that is both rigorous and recreational.

About

Project Brahmagupta is inspired by Project Euler: a recreational space where mathematics and computation meet, with problems released for curious solvers to work through at their own pace. Our focus is the heritage of mathematics in India — its texts, methods, and puzzles — presented with care for history and rigour.

For a short shelf of standard references on the history of mathematics (including works that situate Indian mathematics in the wider story), see References.
